    Scons

    • Preparation time: Less than 30m
    • Cooking time: 10m to 30m
    • Servings: 10
    • Calories: 150 kcal each
    Ingredients Quantity
    Self raising flour 225g/8oz
    Salt 1 pinch
    Butter 55g/2oz
    Caster sugar 25g/1oz
    Milk 150ml/5fl oz
    Free-range eggs 1

    Step 1: Heat the oven to 220C/425F/Gas 7. Lightly grease a baking sheet.

    Step 2: Mix together the flour and salt and rub in the butter.

    Step 3: Stir in the sugar and then the milk to get a soft dough.

    Step 4: Turn on to a floured work surface and knead very lightly. Pat out to a round 2cm/Â¾in thick. Use a 5cm/2in cutter to stamp out rounds and place on a baking sheet. Lightly knead together the rest of the dough and stamp out more scones to use it all up.

    Step 5: Brush the tops of the scones with the beaten egg. Bake for 12-15 minutes until well risen and golden.

    Step 6: Cool on a wire rack and serve with butter and good jam and maybe some clotted cream.

    Source: BBC food

    Luscious Eggnog

    • Preparation time: 15m
    • Cooking time: 15m
    • Servings: 10
    Ingredients Quantity
    Eggs, lightly beaten 3
    White sugar 1/3 cup
    Salt To taste
    Milk 2.5 cups
    White sugar 1.5 cups
    Heavy cream 1 cup
    Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
    Confectioner's sugar 2 tablespoons
    Ground nutmeg 1 pinch

    Step 1: In a heavy saucepan, combine eggs, white sugar, and salt; gradually stir in milk. Stirring constantly, cook for 10 to 15 minutes over medium heat, until mixture just coats a metal spoon. Remove from heat, and stir in 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. Place saucepan over ice water until custard cools, and then refrigerate until chilled.

    Step 2: In a chilled bowl, combine heavy cream, confectioners' sugar, and 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract. Beat until stiff peaks form. Stir in rum and yellow food coloring. Fold whipped cream mixture into the cooled custard. Pour into serving bowl, and sprinkle with nutmeg. Serve immediately.

    Source: Allrecipes

    Cream of Broccoli Soup

    • Time: 1 hour
    • Serves: 4-6
    Ingredients Quantity
    Butter 1 stick (8 tablespoons)
    Flour 2/3 cups
    Onion 1/4 cup
    Broccoli florets cut into bite-sized pieces 4 cups
    Chicken broth 6 tablespoons
    Half-and-half (half cream half milk) 6 tablespoons
    Salt 1 teaspoon
    Pepper 1/4 teaspoon

    Step 1: In a small saucepan, melt 6 tablespoons of butter over low heat. Add in flour and stir until blended. Do no brown.

    Step 2: In a large saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter. Add onion and cook at medium heat until tender. Add in the broccoli florets, and cook and stir for about 6 minutes.

    Step 3: Add chicken broth, increase the heat and bring to a boil, whisking constantly.

    Step 4: Add flour mixture, cook and stir until soup is thick and bubbly.

    Step 5: Lastly, add salt, pepper, and half-and-half. Heat through and serve hot.

    Note: The soup will thicken upon standing. It is possible to substitute cauliflower for broccoli. half-and-half can be substituted by a mixture of equal parts milk and cream.

    Source: Cream of Broccoli Soup by Munchkin Mama
